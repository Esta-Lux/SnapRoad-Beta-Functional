Community Guidelines

Last Updated: May 11, 2026

SnapRoad helps drivers navigate with confidence by combining live traffic, community reports, and safety-first guidance. These Community Guidelines explain how to participate responsibly so the map stays trustworthy, helpful, and respectful for everyone.

Quick Summary: Share only accurate road information, respect other people, never use SnapRoad to harass or endanger anyone, and avoid posting illegal, deceptive, or unsafe content.

1. Put Safety First

  • Never use SnapRoad in a way that distracts you from driving safely or obeying traffic laws.
  • Only create reports, upload media, or interact with the community when it is safe and lawful to do so.
  • Do not encourage reckless driving, speeding, street racing, or evading law enforcement.

2. Share Accurate Reports

  • Submit road reports only when you reasonably believe the information is true and current.
  • Do not spam repeated alerts, fake incidents, prank reports, or intentionally misleading route information.
  • Do not manipulate traffic, offers, rewards, or alert systems with automated, coordinated, or deceptive behavior.

3. Respect Privacy and People

  • Do not post content that reveals someone else’s private information without permission.
  • Do not harass, threaten, intimidate, dox, stalk, or impersonate other users, businesses, or public officials.
  • Keep photos, descriptions, usernames, and feedback respectful and appropriate for a broad public audience.

4. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are not allowed on SnapRoad:

  • Fraud, scams, phishing, or attempts to exploit other users or partners.
  • Hate speech, discrimination, graphic violence, sexual content, or abusive language.
  • Illegal activity, instructions for dangerous acts, or content that promotes self-harm.
  • Malware, scraping, reverse engineering, or attempts to interfere with the app, services, or other users’ accounts.

5. Photos, Media, and Reports

  • Only upload media that you have the right to share.
  • Make sure the content is relevant to a real road, traffic, safety, or travel-related event.
  • Avoid faces, license plates, home addresses, or other identifying details unless they are genuinely necessary and lawfully captured.
Important: SnapRoad may remove reports or media that appear unsafe, misleading, abusive, or unrelated to the road event being reported, even if the post was made without bad intent.

6. Enforcement

If you break these guidelines, we may take action including:

  • Removing content, reports, or uploads
  • Reducing feature access or disabling community privileges
  • Suspending or terminating accounts
  • Reporting serious misconduct to law enforcement or other authorities when required

7. Reporting Problems

If you see abusive behavior, harmful content, or a report that should be reviewed, contact the SnapRoad team so we can investigate.
